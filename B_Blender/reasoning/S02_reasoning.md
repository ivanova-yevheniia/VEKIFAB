# S02 — Assisted Assembly Station — Engineering Reasoning

*VEKIFAB — AI Factory Planner · Compact Medical Infusion Pump (MF-IP-100) · world position [6.0, 6.0, 0.0]*

> Operators build the pump with pick-to-light guidance and torque-controlled tools before robotic handling.

### 1. Why does this station exist?
It performs the dexterous manual assembly of the pump sub-modules that automation cannot yet do cost-effectively.

### 2. Why is it located here?
Second in flow (x=6) directly after loading, so kits arrive with minimal transport.

### 3. Why were these industrial assets selected?
The same profile-frame bench, pick-to-light parts bins, a guidance HMI, task light, cardboard pump trays, cabinet, e-stop, stack light and input/output conveyors.

### 4. Why are the dimensions appropriate?
A 2.5 m bench at 0.90 m gives two operators room to assemble within the 60 s cycle.

### 5. Why is this level of automation used?
Semi-automated: fine assembly stays human, but pick-to-light and torque tools cut error and hold quality.

### 6. Why are these safety systems present?
Emergency stop, stack light and floor markings; the bench is open and low-risk.

### 7. Why is the operator interaction designed this way?
Two operators face the walkway; a guided screwdriver, pick-to-light bins and an HMI showing the build steps reduce mistakes.

### 8. How does this station connect to the previous and next station?
Input conveyor C01 from S01; output conveyor C02 to S03 Robot Cell.

### 9. What assumptions were made?
Manual assembly is feasible within cycle and torque-controlled tools are available.

### 10. What future improvements are possible?
Cobot assistance, poka-yoke fixtures, automated fastening and AR work instructions.
